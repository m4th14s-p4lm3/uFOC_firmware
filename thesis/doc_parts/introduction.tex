\chapter{Introduction}
BLDC motors are increasingly more popular in varous applications, such as robotics, drones, electric vehicles and many more, due to their high efficiency, 
ability to produce large torque at low speeds and high position accuracy. Unlike brushed DC motors, BLDC motors need specialized driver and control to achieve commutation.

While commercial BLDC drivers are available on todays market, such as ODrive Micro, moteus-c1 (add reference), they are often expensive in range of 70-90 USD per driver.
Therefore, the Department of Control Engineering at the Czech Technical University in Prague has developed an 
integrated three-phase motor driver called uFOC (micro FOC) with an STM-32 type microcontroller, which is a low-cost alternative to commercial drivers at BOM price of approximately 20 USD.

To achive an efficient commutation and generate torque control, the driver needs to implement a closed loop control. 
The most common control method for BLDC motors is Field Oriented Control (FOC), 
which allows for precise control of the motor's torque and speed by controlling the current in the stator windings.

Goal of this thesis is to develop embedded firmware that implements FOC current control, speed and position control loops and communication via CAN bus for the uFOC driver. 
Additionally a Python client side example for CAN communication with the driver is provided.

% \chapter{uFOC driver hardware overview}
% The provided uFOC driver contains every component needed for successful implementation of FOC on a very compact area of a signle PCB.
% The driver is based on STM32F302K8U microcontroller, which is a 32-bit ARM Cortex-M4 microcontroller with FPU, running at up to 72 MHz.

% Current control via three PWM outpus from MCU is managed by 3-phase motor driver DRV8316CRRGFR with integrated current sensing connected to three ADCs on the STM32.

% Furthermore, the driver is equipped with MT8635 magnetic encoder with 21 bit accuracy which is used in addition with 
% diametricly oriented magnet on motor shaft to measure absolute position of the rotor. 
% Accurate position reading is crucial input to calculate electrical angle for the FOC control algorithm and instantaneous speed estimation.

% TCAN332 CAN transceiver IC is used to provide communication via CAN bus. Driver is equipped with two CAN connectors, which allows daisy chaining of multiple drivers on a single CAN bus line.

% Additionally, the uFOC driver is equipped with ICM-42670 IMU, however IMU reading is not implemented, as it is not crucial for the FOC control algorithm.
